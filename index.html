About the Seven Wonders

The Philippines is home to some of the most breathtaking natural and man-made wonders in the world. From the iconic Rice Terraces of Banaue to the stunning Tubbataha Reef, these wonders showcase the beauty and diversity of the Philippines.

The Seven Wonders

Rice Terraces of Banaue

Carved by the Ifugao people over 3,000 years ago, these terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Underground River of Puerto Princesa

This UNESCO World Heritage site is the longest navigable underground river in the world.

Tubbataha Reef

One of the best-preserved reef systems, home to over 300 coral species and 400 fish species.

Chocolate Hills

Over 1,200 hills that turn brown during the dry season, located on the island of Bohol.

Taal Volcano

Known for its unique geological formation, Taal is one of the most active volcanoes in the world.

Mayon Volcano

Famous for its perfect cone shape, Mayon is one of the most active volcanoes in the Philippines.

Boracay

Known for its white-sand beaches, Boracay is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines.

Honorable Mentions

El Nido

Known for its stunning limestone cliffs and clear waters, El Nido is a top eco-tourist destination.

Batanes

The northernmost province, known for its unique culture and breathtaking landscapes.

Mall of Asia

One of the largest malls in the world, located in Manila.
